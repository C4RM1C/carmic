UI gem#3 : The hamburger menu

Date: 2/12/18

Author: Clément CARMINATI

We have all seen the hamburger icon. As a matter of fact, most of us probably use it on a daily basis. It has become a staple in website and app design. I’m even looking at it now on the top right corner of Google Chrome.

Lately, there has been much debate about whether the use of the Hamburger Icon and side menus, in general, are really a good idea. But more people have been wondering where it came from and how it got to be so widely used.

The Creator
It was designed by Norm Cox for the Xerox Star, the world’s first graphical user interface. Norm Cox is the co-founder of Cox&Hall and has been an interaction/experience design consultant since 1982. He also happened to design the document icon for the same interface. Geoff Alday is the amazing software designer who went searching for the creator of the hamburger icon and managed to find this video.

First hamburger icon

This was made in 1990, sponsored by the ACM CHI 1990 conference, to tell the history of widgets up until then. Source: Brad Myers on Vimeo
From there he e-mailed mister Norm Cox, who was delighted to share the origin story for the hamburger icon. Here’s what he said:
