Tips for tricks

Date: 24/12/18

Author: Clément CARMINATI

A website has to be relevant, it should have a clear purpose and should provide what the user wants. Every element must add value to the website and follow this idea, if not it must be removed.

Also, clickable elements need to look like they are clickable. Consistency is the key, your content must be easily picked up by your users. Be careful about the tone of voice of your site, it has to reflect your ideas, but the content comes first.

Stick with some design principle: try to make it durable, don't get too obsessed to follow the trend. If you can make a timeless looking website, you're good to go. Sometimes less is more.

Display your content on 12 to 16 vertical columns. Use your space correctly, the important content like images will take more space than your text paragraph.

Format your text correctly, use a large font, put your headers way bigger than the text. And care about line spacing. Try to use a line space that is 50℅ larger than your text size, it will make it more readable. Same with the color choice use a dark font on a light background. Don't use too many different fonts, 3 is more than enough. Paragraphs shouldn't have more than 10 words by line.

Don't be afraid to enlarge your pictures, they have to be large to be properly seen by the user. However be careful with the coping and the quality of the picture, you've better use SVG than compressed images like jpg.

Your logo must be clear, don't make it too edgy

And here comes, the most important stuff, pay attention to detail, don't leave stuff unfinished or broken, you've better remove it. Because if users see that that's the only thing they will remember.
